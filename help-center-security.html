How can I increase security on my smartphone or tablet?

If you bank using your smartphone or tablet, you’re probably wondering how to keep your device as secure as possible. We’ve made a list of tips to help ensure that the banking you do with us on your device is kept private and secure, for your peace of mind.

  • Use your device’s built-in security measures.
    • Lock your device whenever you’re not using it.
    • Set your device to lock automatically after you stop using it.
    • If you can, use a password that’s longer and stronger than the usual 4-digit lock code.
    • Set your device to erase all data after 10 bad password attempts.
  • Take sensitive data off your smartphone or tablet often.
    • Anytime someone else handles your device delete all text messages with us first.
    • If you go to usbank.com for banking on your device, be sure to delete cookies and clear your cache right after.
  • Only download apps after you've read the fine print.
    • If you’re considering adding any app to your mobile device, carefully look over the app’s permissions. That way, you’ll understand what information the app can access before you download it.
  • Know how to avoid phishing scams.
    • Phishers create links that look real, but direct you to malicious websites (which often also look real). If you're not sure about the safety of a website, type the URL in your browser rather than tapping on a link in an email.
  • Delete all personal information before you replace your smartphone or tablet.
    • Practice DIY deleting! Don’t rely on carriers, recyclers or phone deposit banks to “clean” your device when you recycle, upgrade or donate it. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to delete personal information from your device before it leaves your hands for good.
